Get Involved

As a Mp/MPP

MPs, MPPs and other policymakers are the cornerstone of our program - without you, our students would not be able to learn about health policy and politics in a hands-on, authentic experience. As a result, we greatly value your contributions and generosity in time and effort, and endeavour to go above and beyond to make the most of our experiences in your offices.

We are flexible and accommodating to your office’s preferences for scheduling, commitment, timeline, and tasks, and are happy to take on a variety of roles and responsibilities. The ultimate aim of this program would be for the student to produce a “thesis” project by the end of this partnership, ranging from a fully realized public health intervention to a policy briefing on a specific healthcare issue. The details of this can be further determined at the start of the student’s placement.

Depending on your preference and given many of our students’ extensive backgrounds and training in research, we are happy to assist with policy paper/briefings research and writing, as well as utilize our healthcare background for consultations. Many of our students also are experienced in the realms of community advocacy and outreach, and thus we are happy to also aid with stakeholder communications and community outreach collaborations.

Further opportunities to get involved outside of taking on an Apprentice are also available - if you are interested in taking part in events run by us or the larger Canadian medical student community, we are more than happy to facilitate this connection and provide you news of upcoming opportunities.

If you are interested in taking on a medical student apprentice, keeping updated on medical community events/opportunities or have further questions about the role that are unaddressed by our FAQ please feel free to send us an email at mpmdapprenticeship@gmail.com.
