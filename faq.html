F.A.Q.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this be in-person?
We plan to start once the school year starts in September, but the timing as well as duration of the program and frequency of MP-student meetings per month are all up to the preferences of the MP office. We realize that constituency offices may be busy and are more than happy to be very flexible and adaptable with the preferences of MP/MPP offices.
When will this start?
We plan to start once the school year starts in September, but the timing as well as duration of the program and frequency of MP-student meetings per month are all up to the preferences of the MP office. We realize that constituency offices may be busy and are more than happy to be very flexible and adaptable with the preferences of MP/MPP offices.
How long will the apprenticeship run?
The minimum duration of an MD student's commitment to your office is 3 months, with the time commitments within that period of time flexible and determined by your MP/MPP and constituency office's needs. For instance, some offices may want biweekly meetings, while others are happy with weekly or monthly meetings, or close collaboration with policy/research staffers. Your office is free to continue the MD-MPP connection for longer than 3 months if you so choose - the time range can be anywhere from 3 months to 1 school year (ending in May). As medical students, we are very flexible and willing to accommodate the preferences of the MPP office, and are happy to follow the projects/policies assigned to us to its completion should you deem fit.
Will this require students to be paid?
No, we started this program to help students gain exposure, education and experience working with health policy - just as in the spirit of clerkship (a stage in the medical student education journey), where a student shadows a physician to learn, we hope that the experience of essentially shadowing and aiding an policymaker will give the same priceless educational value. In this sense, we are not intended to be summer students nor part-time staffers - we aim primarily to assist with and learn more about healthcare and other policy as per our background in healthcare and research, although we're happy to get involved with logistical matters such as community outreach as well!
What kind of projects will students be assisting with?
Depending on your preference and need, students can be used to engage in policy research, writing briefings/position statements, stakeholder communications and community outreach. The specific details of our role will be flexible according to the needs of each MP/MPP and constituency office.
Why are we not only reaching out to the Health Ministers?
We believe that many, if not all aspects of governance and politics relate back to population health, healthcare and health policy in some way - for instance, environmental policy plays a huge role in diseases like asthma, transportation is increasingly relevant nowadays as we struggle to contain COVID-19 transmission, and economics has a vital role in socioeconomic determinants of health. As a result, our medical students will have plenty to learn from MP/MPPs with any portfolio, and are open to aiding with policy research/writing for health-related and non-health related subjects.
Who are we affiliated with?
We are non-partisan and are currently supported through the volunteer efforts of our executive team made up of medical students across Ontario. We are the recipient of a student initiative grant from the Canadian Federation of Medical Students, but otherwise have no financial disclosures to disclose.
